## What is the SPACE Framework?

The SPACE Framework is a structured instructional model that translates decades of cognitive science research into practical classroom strategies for teachers in low-resource educational settings across Africa and the Global South.

It is built on one core insight: **students remember more when they revisit learning over time — not just once.** The science behind this has been around for over 100 years. The challenge was turning it into something a real teacher could use without overhauling their entire practice, in a classroom with 60 students, no internet, and no budget for apps or platforms.

**No internet. No devices. No specialist software. Just intentional teaching.**

---

## The Five Principles

| Letter | Principle | Core idea |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| **S** | Spaced Retrieval | Return to prior learning across deliberately planned time intervals |
| **P** | Progressive Reinforcement | Strengthen and extend learning cumulatively across sessions |
| **A** | Active Recall | Students reconstruct knowledge from memory — no peeking at notes |
| **C** | Cognitive Consolidation | Space new content so that earlier material has time to solidify |
| **E** | Evidence-Based Adjustment | Track retrieval scores across the term and change what isn't working |

Each principle is designed to function within existing timetable structures — no extra workload for the teacher beyond intentional planning at the start of term.

## Getting Started in Three Steps

You do not need new resources or extra time. These three steps integrate spaced practice into what you already do.

**Step 1 — Map your retrieval points**  
At the start of term, choose four weeks for a 10-minute retrieval task on previous content. Weeks 1, 4, 8, and 12 of a 13-week term are a strong default — the expanding intervals match how memory consolidates.

**Step 2 — Design tasks that require recall**  
Students must produce answers from memory — not recognise them. Paper quizzes, oral questioning, or think-pair-share all work without computers or internet access.

**Step 3 — Record scores and adjust**  
Note class average scores at each retrieval point. If scores at Week 8 are not higher than Week 4, adjust your spacing intervals or task design for the following term.

## Theoretical Foundations

The SPACE Framework synthesises three bodies of cognitive science evidence:

- **Spacing effect** — Distributing practice across time produces stronger long-term retention than massed practice. The optimal gap between sessions scales with how long you want the learning to last *(Cepeda et al., 2006)*.
- **Cognitive load theory** — Spacing reduces cognitive load over time as students build schemas incrementally, freeing working memory for deeper understanding of new content *(Sweller, van Merriënboer & Paas, 1998)*.
- **Retrieval practice** — Active recall — not re-reading or re-watching — is the mechanism that makes spacing work. Effortful retrieval strengthens memory traces far more than passive review *(Roediger & Butler, 2011)*.
